![How We Built a 40-App Portfolio With 50M+ Downloads (And Hit #1 on the App Store Twice) ](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G_N7-pYXEAEcvcz.jpg)# Most app founders chase viral moments. We built a machine.

Over 5 years, my team and I scaled a portfolio of 40+ apps to 50 million downloads. One of our apps a simple habit tracker called Done - hit #1 most downloaded app in the US, ahead of TikTok and Google. Twice.

Here's the entire playbook:

## The Origin: UGC Before It Was Cool

In 2017, Reflectly was launched an AI journaling app before AI was trendy. By 2020, we'd scaled it to $3-4M ARR.

The secret? We discovered UGC arbitrage on Facebook ads when everyone else was running polished graphics.

We found micro-influencers in local markets, had them speak in their native dialect, and ran their "ugly" videos as ads. Bad lighting, babies crying in the background these always outperformed.

When we couldn't predict which videos would win, we stopped trying to predict. We built a system instead.

## The Factory: 250 Creators, One University

We tried agencies. None of them cracked it.

So we built our own creator army. At peak, we had 250 regular creators (cycled through 2,000+ total). But we didn't just hire them we trained them.

We built an in-house university teaching:

- How the algorithm actually works
- What metrics matter (not just views - conversion metrics)
- The specific frame count that drives engagement (25-27 cuts in a 10-15 second video)
- How to read comments to predict virality

The deal: Learn our playbook, make videos for us, then use those skills to charge bigger brands 3x more. Everyone won.

## The Three-Tier Scaling System

Every video followed the same path:

**Level 1: The Test**

- Every video gets $15-20 in ad spend
- After 1-2 days, we know if it's working
- Most die here after $5-10

**Level 2: The Qualifier**

- Winners graduate to $100-500 budgets
- Fewer videos, higher stakes
- TikTok tests them at new scale

**Level 3: The Evergreens**

- Top 1% of videos
- These get 90% of total ad spend
- Sometimes the same video runs at all three levels simultaneously

## The January Arbitrage

Here's what most people miss: timing matters more than creative.

The first week of January is the Super Bowl of health and fitness apps. Cost per download drops 50%. Conversion rates double.

We spent 75% of our entire annual ad budget in January alone.

But it gets more granular:

- Dec 25: Dead
- Dec 26-28: Start testing at $2-3K/day
- Dec 28-30: Conversion jumps, increase spend
- Jan 1-3: Go all in

One more thing: if January 1st falls on a Thursday or Friday, the real peak is that Sunday. People don't mentally commit to resolutions until the week "starts."

## The Machine: End-to-End Creator Operations

Here's what nobody talks about: the operational system behind the content.

We didn't just "work with creators." We built a fully automated pipeline that ran like a factory:

**1. Sourcing**

Reached out to thousands of creators via DM and email. Target: 2,000-50,000 followers. We wanted content creators, not influencers. We're paying for video production skills, not audience reach.

**2. Interviewing**

We built a landing page specifically to recruit creators - complete with testimonials from existing creators about working with us. If they couldn't follow basic application instructions, they wouldn't follow briefs either.

**3. Onboarding**

Every creator went through our "university." They learned our frameworks, our metrics, our expectations. We taught them what makes content convert not just go viral.

**4. Test Videos**

First assignment: a basic day-in-the-life video following our guidelines. Can they execute? Can they hit deadlines? This filtered out 90% of applicants.

**5. Content Assignment**

Qualified creators got assigned to specific apps in the portfolio based on their style and our content needs. We allocated thousands of videos across 20+ apps based on performance data.

**6. Review & Revise**

Every video went through editing feedback loops. We were specific (at first): frame count, hook timing, phone angle, sound selection. Back and forth until it hit our standards. Once they understood the guardrails, that's when they could open up to even more creative freedom.

**7. Approve & Push to Ads**

Approved videos went straight into Level 1 campaigns. No delays. The system was built for speed.

**8. Feedback Loop**

Performance data flowed back to creators. They saw what worked and what didn't. Their rates increased as their hit rate improved. $30-40/video for beginners, up to $80-100 for proven performers, plus bonuses for viral hits.

**9. Repeat**

The whole cycle ran continuously. While one batch of videos was in ads, the next batch was in production, and we were onboarding the next wave of creators.

We automated every step: invoicing, payments, brief distribution, performance tracking. A team of three (me, a media buyer, and a head of content) managed 250 active creators producing thousands of videos.

That's the real moat. Not one viral video - a system that produces them reliably.

## The Creator Brief That Actually Works

We didn't let creators freestyle. We gave them surgical instructions:

**Requirements:**

- 10-15 seconds
- 25-27 cuts minimum
- POV shots looking down at phone
- Trending sound (we paid someone to compile weekly lists)

**Four video types:**

- Lifestyle (morning/evening routines)
- Trend-based (current sounds/formats)
- Educational
- Testimonial

**Hook frameworks:**

- Curiosity-based
- Empathetic
- Reflective

We even paid someone to give us a weekly report on trending sounds/hooks and video styles so we could jump on the trend as soon as possible.

## The Unexplainable Magic

Here's the honest truth: our best creator made no sense.

One woman consistently hit 10's+ million views. We tried replicating her exact videos with 40 different creators. Nothing worked. She'd post another video viral again.

We analyzed frame counts, hooks, everything. Still can't explain it.

The lesson? Build a system that tests thousands of videos so you can find the outliers you could never predict.

## What I'd Tell You Now

The game has changed. Creators are more sophisticated. The arbitrage windows are smaller.

But the fundamentals haven't:

- Build systems, not bets
- Test thousands of videos to find the few that work
- Time your spend to market tailwinds
- Train your creators like employees, not vendors
- Read the comments before the view counts

We sold most of our app portfolio and now I'm building [Fload](https://fload.com?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=helloryanjarticle), AI agents that run your entire mobile growth playbook. Because the next era of app growth isn't about working harder. It's about building systems that work while you sleep.

*This is what we learned buying, operating and growing 30+ apps to 50M downloads. The playbook is yours.*
