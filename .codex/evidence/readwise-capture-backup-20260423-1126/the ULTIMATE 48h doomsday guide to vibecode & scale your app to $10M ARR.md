## **The Great B2B vs. B2C Delusion**

People in the tech bubble keep telling you to build B2B because "*churn is lower,*" "*it's easier to sell,*" and "*B2C users are broke.*"

So what do you do? You spend three months building a $29/month analytics dashboard, a social media scheduler, or a niche CRM for indie hackers.

Here is the brutal truth: **$29/month is B2C pricing. You are already doing B2C, you are just doing it on hard mode.**

Indie hackers and tech founders are the absolute worst user base. They are hyper-picky, have a chronic "I could build this myself in a weekend" mentality, are allergic to paying, and will constantly compare your polished tool to a free open-source GitHub repo.

A subscription model doesn’t make it B2B. The user’s psychological state makes it B2B.

A true B2B customer pays thousands because your tool explicitly prints them money or saves them headcount. A B2C customer pays $4.99 a week because your app makes them *feel* something: better, hotter, calmer, more confident, less anxious, or more productive.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HEC0UlDaoAACY9Y.jpg)

Mobile is cracked right now. We are entering a massive boom in the mobile app economy. AI makes it ridiculously easy to build a functional app in seven days, and AI UGC gives you the keys to infinite, zero-cost distribution.

If you don't know Swift, aren't a UI/UX designer, and have absolutely no idea what to build—perfect. That is the exact starting line.

## **Phase 1: Idea Generation (Without Being a Genius)**

If you aren't chronically online, your first step is mandatory: **download TikTok.**

You aren't scrolling to fry your dopamine receptors. You are scrolling 10 minutes a day to study human psychology. You are looking for what Gen Z complains about, what they flex, what makes them deeply insecure, and what they wish they were doing.

Your browser does not support the video tag.Trends equal demand. And demand is the only metric that matters.

Let's look at a real example. I found a massive trend called “underconsumption core,” where people are suddenly romanticizing the act of spending less money and keeping things minimal. If you search that keyword, you’ll find hundreds of thousands of videos.

Here is the alpha: **Do not build an “underconsumption core app.”** Build an app that solves the emotional bleeding *behind* the trend. Solve for: *“I spend money when I’m anxious,”* *“I buy stuff to cope,”* or *“I need to gamify saving money so I don't feel broke.”* That is how you extract a million-dollar app idea from a teenager's vlog.

**3 Guaranteed Idea Templates:**

- **Template A: The Tracker.** Spending tracker, habit tracker, mood tracker, time tracker. Gen Z is obsessed with watching numbers go up or down. Give them a dashboard for their life.
- **Template B: The Coach.** Daily prompts, 7-day shadow-work challenges, "level up" systems. They don't want blank utility tools; they want guided authority.
- **Template C: Aesthetic Utility.** Minimalist notes, pretty timers, cute checklists. They want vibes + function.

*Pro Tip:* Search the word “app” in TikTok comments. When users comment, “I need this as an app,” that is a literal, flashing buy signal.

## **Phase 2: The UI/UX Heist (Stealing Like an Artist)**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HD97KKGWoAEDOAT.jpg)

We are not designers. We do not know Figma. So we *leverage the companies that have already spent millions of dollars on conversion rate optimization and user psychology.*

If you are building an aesthetic budgeting app, **download the top 10 finance and self-improvement apps on the store.**

Screenshot everything. Screenshot the first onboarding screen. Screenshot the paywall flow. Screenshot the settings menu. Screenshot the exact "aha moment" where the app finally delivers value.

You are not copying their graphic design; you are copying their **proven behavior design.** **Gen Z UI Cheat Codes:**

- **Dark mode by default.** (Non-negotiable).
- **Massive typography.** (Make it loud).
- **Minimal text.** (Nobody reads paragraphs; use punchy micro-copy).
- **Emojis in headings.** (Keep it native, but don't overdo it).
- **Progress bars everywhere.** (Visual dopamine triggers).
- **Streaks / Levels / Badges.** (If they lose their 12-day streak, they will open the app).

## **Phase 3: Vibecoding the Engine**

I’ve lived in the terminal and tested everything from Cursor to Claude Code for complex logic and backend architecture. But when you need to rapidly vibecode a mobile frontend and get an MVP in your hands today, my go-to is **Rork**.

You’ve seen their goated marketing on X. But the tool is actually lethal if you know how to prompt it.

Take your stack of competitor screenshots and your core idea, and feed them into a high-tier reasoning model (like me, Gemini 3.1 Pro, explicitly designed to handle heavy multimodal UI/UX analysis).

**The Master Prompt:**

*"I am building a mobile app: [Describe your idea and the emotional problem it solves]. Analyze these attached screenshots. Describe every detail of the UI/UX and user flow, and adapt it to my idea. I want it to be structurally similar, but improved to fit a Gen Z audience with high aesthetic standards. My ultimate goal is [Core Benefit]. Output the complete navigation structure, the exact onboarding flow to get them to the paywall, core MVP features, and one highly sticky habit loop to increase daily retention. Finally, generate the exact, comprehensive prompt I need to feed into Rork to build this."*

Do not ask the AI to "build an app." Ask it to act as a **Principal Product Designer** focusing strictly on friction removal and retention triggers. Once you have the architecture, you feed that directly into Rork.
