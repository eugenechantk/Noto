I scaled my app from 0 to $150K/month in just 8 months. The app itself? Pretty dumb and silly, it's a height app.

This article is my reflection of what I did to pick the right idea, and my framework for picking my next app idea.

## You should be passionate about your business, but what is passion?

Real passion comes from **excelling** at the thing, not just loving the subject.

You can love an idea in theory. I've built games and productivity apps that I loved.

But after spending a year building your “passion project," Motivation tanks no matter how much you liked the concept.

So flip it:

Pick an idea where you think you can succeed with. Passion = competence.

## You Must go into Markets With Competition

First let's dispel a myth.

**STARTUPS DO NOT DIE BECAUSE OF COMPETITION**

It's statistically a fact that most startups die due no PMF and the founder giving up. At the early stages, there are so many things a founder can fuck up that execution is the only thing that matters, especially if your competition is other startups.

If there is no competition? Run.

My app entered a space with tons of players. I even had a big competitor who made over $100K a month, tried to steal all my UGC creators one day by offering them more compensation. I not only survived it, I thrived after by being forced to increase my ARPU to compete and create better creator relationships.

# Still choose topics you are "passionate" about

If you hate the niche so much you can’t even stomach the content in that space (for me I cannot stand watching through BP edits), you are gonna lose to the guy who actually consumes content in the niche

The only way to compensate for this is to find a cofounder who is heavily interested in the subject area, but it helps a lot if you’re at least adjacent.

To explain why I was interested in the height niche, I knew that the app idea would work based off my own experience as a short 13 year old. I haven't consumed any looksmaxxing and certainly heightmaxxing content in *years*, but understanding the anxiety people feel firsthand as well as the topics, helped me craft a script and onboarding that I estimate to be double that of my larger competitor at the time.

Best case scenario:

- You’ve personally faced the problem
- You already consume the content
- You lurk the communities

That gives you instant advantage in product intuition and marketing.

## The Edge People Forget

I love consumer apps.

You get to choose how degenerate you are with how you run the company, the content you push, the features, etc...

Our app is ridiculous and we get the funniest convos with influencers and users, I post some of them on my profile and ppl love it.

If I were stuck in enterprise B2B SaaS with contracts, compliance, and endless demos? I likely would've burnt out

But I have so many fun memories of working on the app and just laughing along with my cofounders on whatever funny shit happened that day.

Some people thrive on boring: spreadsheets, long sales cycles, predictable revenue.

For me? I need some degeneracy and fun to stay motivated.

## The Quick Idea Filter

Stack these filters:

- You’ve lived the pain (or close to it)
- You have some unfair advantage (skills, audience, network, taste)
- There’s clear competition (proof of demand)
- You actually will enjoy running this kind of business

I didn't talk about TAM, but honestly, use your best judgement. Apps don't succeed either because there's 0 TAM or no execution. TAM only matters if you want to do your app for years. But otherwise, save that stuff to pitch to the VCs, not to make cold hard cash.
