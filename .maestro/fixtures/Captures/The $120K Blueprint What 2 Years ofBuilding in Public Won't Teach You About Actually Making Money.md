## Insights

Context: this is a service business (design agency), not an app — "idea discovery" maps to offer positioning; distribution is organic content + DM outreach.

### Idea Discovery (offer discovery)

1. **Sell transformation, not skills.** He didn't sell "dashboard design" or "UI/UX services." He sold *clarity*. Positioning: "90% of SaaS products look like cluttered spreadsheets. I make them look like $500M enterprise brands." Formula: call out the problem → promise the transformation → position yourself as the solution.
2. **Diagnose before you prescribe — the real idea comes from reframing the client's stated need.** "We need a redesign" usually means "We need to look credible to investors." The product/offer lives in the reframe, not the stated request. Listen for the emotional/business outcome underneath the surface ask.
3. **Same skill, different platform = different product.** His design ability didn't change from Behance to X. What changed was what he was selling on each: Behance rewarded execution (views/features = $1K projects), X rewarded taste/vision/opinion ($3.5K–$30K). Your "product" is shaped by the platform's dominant currency.
4. **Pricing is a positioning signal.** Price should make you slightly uncomfortable. Too cheap signals low worth. Evolution: $1K → $3.5K → $7.5K → $15K → $30K/month retainer. "The projects didn't change. My positioning did."

### Early Distribution

**Platform shift — the biggest lever.**
- Go where the buyers spend time, not where peers are.
- Choose a platform that rewards provocative opinions over polished portfolios (taste is rare; polish is abundant).
- His shift: Behance (views/features) → X (belief/conviction). 10× revenue.

**The 3-Post Daily Content System.** Every day, post three pieces in sequence:
1. **Provocation (morning)** — controversial take challenging conventional wisdom. Purpose: position yourself as someone with taste. Example: *"Not every website needs gradients to feel premium."*
2. **Education (afternoon)** — teach something that reframes the audience's assumptions. Purpose: establish authority + educate future buyers. Example: *"90% of SaaS founders: 'we just need something simple.' This is what simple actually looks like."*
3. **Proof (evening)** — show your work with a twist that demonstrates your unique approach. Purpose: prove you can deliver on the provocation. Example: *"It's funny how everyone is in disbelief that I used Figma for my designs."*

**Sequence logic:** Provocation hooks them → Education explains why the provocation is right → Proof shows you can deliver. Each post reinforces the others.

**Raw beats polished.** His most viral posts were vulnerable/real, not curated. Origin post ("I've been saying I'm starting my agency since 2023…") got 9K views but signaled the shift from performing to being real. That tone enabled the later viral hits (500K+ views).

**The Engagement System — 4 hours daily.**
- **Morning (1 hr):** 20 thoughtful comments on posts from designers/founders with 1K–10K followers (peer/adjacent layer)
- **Afternoon (1 hr):** reply to every comment on your own posts — turn comments into conversations
- **Evening (2 hr):** comment on posts from 50K+ follower accounts to get surfaced to their audience
- Takeaway: *engagement matters more than followers.* Most people post and ghost; the unglamorous hours are where the moat is.

**The Sales/DM System** (what converts inbound interest into paid work):
1. Don't pitch. Ask questions ("What are you building?" "Biggest challenge with your current design?" "What would success look like?")
2. Diagnose before prescribing — listen for the real ask underneath the stated one
3. Reframe their problem in your words; when they say "yes, exactly," the sale is made
4. Present the solution as inevitable — don't ask "does this work?" or "are you interested?" Assume the sale, move straight to logistics ("Send over your brand assets, we'll start Monday"). This alone moved his close rate from 20% → 80%.

**Outbound DM template:**
"Hey [name], saw your [specific thing]. Quick question: are you happy with how [specific aspect] is positioned? I've helped [similar company type] go from [problem] to [result], and noticed a few things about [their product] that could be optimized. Mind if I send over a quick visual breakdown?"
Offer value (a visual breakdown), not a pitch.

**30-day distribution sprint (the execution cadence):**
- Week 1: positioning + platform setup
- Week 2: content engine (3 posts/day + 4hr engagement)
- Week 3: start outbound DMs (2 hrs/day, 20 DMs/day)
- Week 4: close deals (assume the sale)

### Net-Net (translatable to apps/products)

- **Platform choice determines what you're really selling.** Optimize for belief/conviction on the platform where your buyer is, not views on the platform where your peers are.
- **Provocation → Education → Proof** is a reusable content triad that works for any category — not just design.
- **Engagement is leverage, not posting.** The 4-hour comment loop is what separates creators with reach from creators with income.
- **Reframe the stated problem into the real problem** — both in marketing copy and in sales conversations. The real product sits one layer beneath what customers verbalize.
